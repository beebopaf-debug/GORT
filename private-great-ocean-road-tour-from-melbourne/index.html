Comfort and pacing

A More Comfortable Way to See the Great Ocean Road

A public coach tour can be a practical lower-cost option, but it usually follows a fixed schedule with a larger group. Self-driving gives independence, but one person still has to manage a long day of driving, parking, navigation, and fatigue.

A private Great Ocean Road tour gives your group a more comfortable way to experience the region, with private transport, door-to-door convenience, and more flexibility around the stops that matter most to you.

Sample route

Sample Great Ocean Road Day Tour Itinerary

The final route depends on your pick-up point, daylight, weather, road conditions, traffic, and your preferred pace.

Morning: Melbourne to the Surf Coast

Private pick-up from your Melbourne accommodation or agreed meeting point, then travel toward the Surf Coast with possible stops such as the Memorial Arch and early coastal viewpoints.

Midday: Coastal Towns and Lookouts

The day may include stops around Lorne, Apollo Bay, selected scenic lookouts, and a lunch or rest break depending on timing.

Afternoon: Twelve Apostles, Loch Ard Gorge and Gibson Steps

The afternoon is usually focused around Port Campbell National Park and the famous limestone coastline.

Return: Comfortable Drive Back to Melbourne

The return route is usually planned for comfort and timing rather than repeating the full coastal road.

Highlights

Possible Tour Highlights

A private tour may include a selection of the following highlights. Not every stop can be included comfortably on every tour.

Loch Ard Gorge

A dramatic coastal gorge with powerful scenery and one of the region's most famous shipwreck stories.

Gibson Steps

A beach-level viewpoint beneath towering limestone cliffs, available when access and conditions allow.

Maits Rest Rainforest

A short rainforest walk through the Otways, included when timing and conditions suit the day.
